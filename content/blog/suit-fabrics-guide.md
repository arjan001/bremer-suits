The fabric of your suit determines how it looks, feels, drapes, and endures. Understanding textiles is one of the most valuable investments you can make in your personal style.

## Wool: The King of Suiting

Wool is the most versatile suiting fabric and the foundation of most bespoke wardrobes. It breathes in warm weather, insulates in cold weather, resists wrinkles, and drapes beautifully.

- **Super 100s-120s**: Durable and ideal for everyday wear
- **Super 130s-150s**: Softer and more luxurious, suited for special occasions
- **Super 160s+**: Exceptionally fine but delicate — handle with care

## Linen: The Warm-Weather Essential

Linen is a natural fibre prized for its breathability and relaxed texture. It wrinkles naturally, which is part of its charm. A linen suit in cream, sky blue, or olive is the perfect warm-climate choice.

## Cotton: The Casual Workhorse

Cotton suits offer comfort and a structured look. They're ideal for smart-casual occasions and warmer months. Cotton chinos paired with a cotton blazer create a polished yet relaxed silhouette.

## Silk: The Statement Maker

Silk adds sheen and luxury to any garment. Often blended with wool, silk creates suits that shimmer subtly under light — ideal for evening events and celebrations.

## Blends: The Best of Both Worlds

Modern suiting often combines fibres for optimal performance. Wool-silk blends offer luxury with structure, wool-linen blends provide breathability with drape, and wool-cashmere blends deliver softness with warmth.

## How to Choose

Consider three factors:

1. **Climate**: Where will you wear this suit most?
2. **Occasion**: Formal events demand finer fabrics; daily wear needs durability
3. **Personal preference**: Touch the fabric. If it doesn't feel right, it won't feel right on your shoulders.
