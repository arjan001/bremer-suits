Your clothing is a form of non-verbal communication. Before you speak a single word, your appearance has already sent a message. Understanding this dynamic is the first step toward using style as a strategic tool.

## The Science of First Impressions

Research suggests that people form judgments about competence, trustworthiness, and status within seconds of seeing someone. Clothing plays an outsized role in these snap assessments. A well-dressed individual is consistently rated as more competent and more trustworthy.

## Enclothed Cognition

Studies in "enclothed cognition" show that what you wear doesn't just affect how others see you — it changes how you think and behave. Participants wearing formal clothing demonstrated increased abstract thinking and broader perspectives. When you dress well, you think differently.

## Dressing for Your Context

The key insight from image consulting isn't that you should always dress formally. It's that you should dress *intentionally* for your context. A tech founder and a corporate attorney need different wardrobes, but both benefit from clothes that are chosen with purpose rather than by default.

## Making It Work for You

Start by understanding the visual codes of your industry and role. Then, find ways to express your personality within those parameters. The goal isn't to conform blindly — it's to communicate clearly while remaining authentically you.
