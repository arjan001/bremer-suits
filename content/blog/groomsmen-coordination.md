Coordinating your groomsmen's attire is one of the more nuanced decisions in wedding planning. The goal is cohesion without uniformity — a wedding party that looks intentional, not like a uniform lineup.

## The Spectrum of Coordination

There are several approaches to groomsmen styling, and the right one depends on your wedding's formality and aesthetic:

**Full matching**: Everyone wears the same suit, shirt, and tie. Best for formal, traditional weddings.

**Same suit, varied accessories**: The suits match, but each groomsman chooses their own tie, pocket square, or boutonniere. This adds personality while maintaining a clean look.

**Colour palette matching**: Each groomsman wears a different suit within the same colour family (shades of blue, for example). Modern and visually dynamic.

**Mix and match**: Each groomsman wears their own suit in a complementary style. Works best for smaller, relaxed wedding parties.

## Practical Considerations

- **Body types vary**: What looks great on one groomsman may not flatter another. Made-to-measure or bespoke options ensure everyone looks their best.
- **Budget matters**: Be upfront about costs and consider options at various price points. Rental is an option, but a purchased suit has lasting value.
- **Comfort counts**: Your groomsmen will be standing, dancing, and celebrating. Choose fabrics and fits that allow movement and handle the venue's climate.

## The Groom Should Stand Out

Subtle distinctions set the groom apart. A different suit colour, a unique lapel style, a distinctive tie or boutonniere — small differences communicate your role without shouting it.

## Accessories as Unifiers

If the suits differ, accessories can tie the look together. Matching ties, lapel pins, cufflinks, or even socks create visual cohesion across the party.
